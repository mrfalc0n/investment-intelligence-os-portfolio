# Synthetic Stock Research Brief

## TL;DR

This example shows how a thesis-aware research agent can classify a signal without promoting it directly into an action queue.

## Research Signal

A synthetic grid-infrastructure supplier reported higher backlog tied to data-center power upgrades.

## Thesis Mapping

| Lens | Assessment |
|---|---|
| AI infrastructure | Relevant: power availability is a bottleneck for compute growth. |
| Grid equipment | Relevant: backlog may support long-duration demand. |
| Valuation | Unknown: requires review before classification improves. |
| Source quality | Medium: needs primary filing or transcript confirmation. |
| Portfolio relevance | Watchlist / radar. |

## Classification

```text
classification: Watchlist / Radar
portfolio_action: false
thesis_relook: false
candidate_review: possible_after_source_confirmation
```

## Researcher Notes

- This is a useful signal, not a recommendation.
- Confirm from a primary source before upgrading.
- Map suppliers, customers, and adjacent holdings before any allocation discussion.
- Route to weekly thesis research if corroborated.

## Human Review Question

Does this signal indicate a broad thesis reinforcement, or only a company-specific backlog event?
